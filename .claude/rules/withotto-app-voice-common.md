# Voice and writing: shared conventions

Conventions that apply across every page and post on withotto.app. Load alongside the page-type-specific file: `withotto-app-voice-marketing.md` for landing, product, and pricing pages, or `withotto-app-voice-blog.md` for blog posts.

## Audience

The primary audience is UK accountants and bookkeepers running or working in practices, looking at tools to reduce manual work. They are not looking for hype. They are looking for something that will actually save them time and not cause more problems than it solves.

A secondary audience is business users who do their own bookkeeping. Less technical about accounting but still skim critically and still hate marketing fluff.

Write for people who are intelligent, time-poor, and sceptical of SaaS marketing.

## Otto (the persona)

Otto is a mascot: a friendly, veteran bookkeeper character used in illustrations and copy. Otto is not a real person. Otto doesn't attend events, write code, or send emails. When describing what Otto "does" in the product, keep it to what the software actually does: reading documents, matching transactions, suggesting codes, publishing bills.

Otto is third person ("Otto reads each receipt", "Otto matches transactions") by default across the site. For the narrow exception where Otto speaks in first person, see `withotto-app-voice-marketing.md`.

## "We" (the team)

"We" refers to With Otto the company. Use "we" for:

- Decisions about the product ("We don't charge minimums because…")
- Commitments to the customer ("We respond to support emails within a working day")
- Technical infrastructure ("We host in AWS London")
- Honest limitations ("Xero certification is in progress")

Don't use "we" as a synonym for Otto. If the software does something, Otto or the product is the subject, not "we".

## Voice and tone

Professional, warm, and measured. Transparent and honest, a deliberate counterpoint to typical SaaS marketing. No overselling. Let complexity speak for itself rather than being overtly critical of competitors.

Anti-pushy in sales communications. Customer-first even at revenue cost.

Sentences should be short, direct, active. Readers should feel a human wrote this. If a sentence sounds like it came from a landing-page template, rewrite it.

## Conversational moves

Do:

- Address the reader as "you". Address practices as "your practice" or "your team".
- Name specific problems the reader actually has ("chasing clients for missing receipts", "reconciling 400 transactions on the first of the month").
- Cite concrete facts where we have them (per-document pricing, no minimums, line items by default).
- Admit limitations honestly (beta status, Xero certification in progress, wind-down for Otto Bank Rec).

Avoid:

- "Revolutionise", "transform", "game-changing", "cutting-edge", "next-generation", "world-class".
- "Seamlessly", "effortlessly", "easily" as filler.
- "Unleash", "unlock", "supercharge".
- "We believe" without evidence.
- Passive voice in marketing prose ("transactions are reconciled" → "Otto reconciles transactions").

## Punctuation and typography

- **No em dashes.** Use commas, "until", or split the sentence. Strictly enforced.
- **En dashes for ranges only:** "22:00–23:00", "2024–2026".
- **Serial comma.** "Xero, FreeAgent, and QuickBooks."
- **Colons introduce definitions and examples**, not dramatic reveals.
- **Exclamation marks sparingly.** At most one per page, only where genuine.

## UK English

"Organisation" not "organization". "Recognise" not "recognize". "Colour" not "color". Dates in day-month-year format ("24 April 2026" not "04/24/2026"). Currency in GBP by default (£); include the code if another currency appears. British quotation conventions: double for direct quotes, single for nested, punctuation outside quotes unless it's part of the quoted material.

## Numbers, dates, times, money

Cambridge rule: spell out numbers under 10, numerals for 10 and above. Always numerals for:

- Percentages: "3% of transactions"
- Currency: "£25 per month", "£0.10 per document"
- Precise times: "9:00", "22:00–23:00"
- Technical counts: "12 months of data", "100 documents per month"

## Headings

Sentence case everywhere. "Pricing plans", not "Pricing Plans". Proper nouns retain their capitalisation ("Xero", "FreeAgent", "Passkeys"). One H1 per page. Avoid going deeper than H3 on marketing pages.

## Links

Descriptive link text. "Download the pricing spreadsheet" is good. "Click here" is not. External links to trusted third parties are fine (AWS, Supabase, Xero). No affiliate-style or tracking-heavy outbound links.

## Phrases to avoid

| Avoid                        | Use                                                      |
| ---------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------- |
| Our cutting-edge AI          | Otto or the specific action ("Otto extracts line items") |
| Game-changing                | A concrete claim with numbers                            |
| Revolutionise, transform     | A specific outcome                                       |
| Seamlessly, effortlessly     | Omit, or describe what actually happens                  |
| World-class support          | "Support from the people who built it", or omit          |
| Best-in-class                | Omit. If true, show it; don't claim it                   |
| Next-generation              | Omit                                                     |
| Trusted by [vague claim]     | Name specific customers, or omit                         |
| Unleash, unlock, supercharge | The actual verb for what happens                         |
